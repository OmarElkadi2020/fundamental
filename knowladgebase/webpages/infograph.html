The Lynch Method: A Story-Driven Approach

Peter Lynch championed a "bottom-up" strategy, leveraging common sense and categorizing stocks to find opportunities missed by Wall Street.

Lynch's Portfolio Focus

Lynch believed the greatest returns—"tenbaggers"—came from a specific category. This chart shows a conceptual breakdown of where he focused his search for life-changing investments.

Fast Growers

Small, agile companies with >20% annual earnings growth. The highest risk and reward.

Stalwarts

Large, stable companies (e.g., Coca-Cola) growing at 10-12%. Offer protection in recessions.

Cyclicals

Profits ebb and flow with the economy (e.g., auto, airlines). Timing is everything.

Turnarounds

Beaten-down companies with potential for recovery. High risk, high potential return.

Asset Plays

Companies with valuable hidden assets (e.g., real estate, cash) overlooked by the market.

Slow Growers

Large, mature companies growing slowly. Primarily for dividend income.

A Tale of Two Titans: Trader vs. Owner

While both sought growth, their core philosophies diverged significantly. O'Neil acted as a systematic trader, while Lynch was a long-term business owner.
